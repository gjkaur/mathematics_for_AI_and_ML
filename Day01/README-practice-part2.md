<h2 id="sec1">1. Inverse of a matrix</h2>

### Q1

Find the inverse of

```math
A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 & 0 \\ 2 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\,.
```

**Concept.** The **inverse** $A^{-1}$ “undoes” $A$. It exists only when $A$ is **nonsingular** (full rank, $\det(A)\neq 0$). If one row is a combination of others, $A$ is singular.

**Solution (step by step).**

1. **Row dependence.** $\mathrm{row}_1-\mathrm{row}_2=[2,1,1]=\mathrm{row}_3$, so $\mathrm{row}_1-\mathrm{row}_2-\mathrm{row}_3=\mathbf{0}$.

2. **Conclusion.** $\mathrm{rank}(A)\le 2$, so $\det(A)=0$. **No inverse.**

3. **Optional determinant (cofactor expansion along row 1):**

```math
\det(A)=3\begin{vmatrix}1&0\\1&1\end{vmatrix}-2\begin{vmatrix}1&0\\2&1\end{vmatrix}+1\begin{vmatrix}1&1\\2&1\end{vmatrix}=3-2-1=0\,.
```

---

### Q2

Determine whether the following $A$ is invertible; if yes, find $A^{-1}$:

```math
A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 2 & 4 & 6 \\ 1 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\,.
```

**Concept.** Invertible $\Leftrightarrow$ independent rows. If one row is a **multiple** of another, $\det(A)=0$.

**Solution (step by step).**

1. $\mathrm{row}_2=[2,4,6]=2[1,2,3]=2\cdot\mathrm{row}_1$.

2. **Not invertible**; no $A^{-1}$.

<h2 id="sec2">2. Eigenvalues and eigenvectors</h2>

### Q3

Find the real eigenvalues and eigenvectors of

```math
A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\,.
```

**Concept.** Eigenvectors satisfy $A\mathbf{v}=\lambda\mathbf{v}$. Eigenvalues solve $\det(A-\lambda I)=0$; eigenvectors lie in $\ker(A-\lambda I)$.

**Solution (step by step).**

1. **Characteristic polynomial:**

```math
\det(A-\lambda I)=(2-\lambda)^2-1=\lambda^2-4\lambda+3=(\lambda-1)(\lambda-3)\,.
```

Eigenvalues: $\lambda_1=1$, $\lambda_2=3$.

2. **$\lambda=1$:** $A-I=\begin{bmatrix}1&1\\1&1\end{bmatrix}$ gives $v_1+v_2=0$. Take $\mathbf{v}=\begin{bmatrix}1\\-1\end{bmatrix}$.

3. **$\lambda=3$:** $A-3I=\begin{bmatrix}-1&1\\1&-1\end{bmatrix}$ gives $v_2=v_1$. Take $\mathbf{v}=\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix}$.

---

### Q4

Find eigenvalues and eigenvectors of

```math
A = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -2 \\ 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\,.
```

**Concept.** Same as Q3: characteristic equation, then nullspace of $A-\lambda I$ for each $\lambda$.

**Solution (step by step).**

1.

```math
\det(A-\lambda I)=(4-\lambda)(1-\lambda)+2=\lambda^2-5\lambda+6=(\lambda-2)(\lambda-3)\,.
```

Eigenvalues $2$ and $3$.

2. **$\lambda=2$:** $A-2I=\begin{bmatrix}2&-2\\1&-1\end{bmatrix}$ $\Rightarrow$ $v_1=v_2$. Eigenvector $\begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix}$.

3. **$\lambda=3$:** $A-3I=\begin{bmatrix}1&-2\\1&-2\end{bmatrix}$ $\Rightarrow$ $v_1=2v_2$. Eigenvector $\begin{bmatrix}2\\1\end{bmatrix}$.

---

### Q5

Is the following matrix diagonalizable?

```math
A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1 \\ 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\,.
```

**Concept.** Diagonalizable means a **full basis of eigenvectors**. Repeated eigenvalues need enough eigenvectors: **geometric** multiplicity $=$ **algebraic** multiplicity.

**Solution (step by step).**

1. Triangular $A$: eigenvalue $\lambda=3$ with algebraic multiplicity $2$.

2. $(A-3I)=\begin{bmatrix}0&1\\0&0\end{bmatrix}$ $\Rightarrow$ $v_2=0$, $v_1$ free. Eigenspace spanned by $\begin{bmatrix}1\\0\end{bmatrix}$ — **one** dimension.

3. Geometric $1<2$ $\Rightarrow$ **not diagonalizable** (Jordan block).

<h2 id="sec3">3. Rank of a matrix</h2>

### Q6

Find the rank of

```math
A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 2 & 4 & 6 \\ 1 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\,.
```

**Concept.** **Rank** $=$ number of pivot rows after Gaussian elimination $=$ dimension of row space.

**Solution (step by step).**

1. $R_2\leftarrow R_2-2R_1$ gives a zero row.

2. $R_3\leftarrow R_3-R_1$: $[0,-1,-2]$.

3. Echelon form (reorder):

```math
\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\,.
```

Two pivots $\Rightarrow$ **$\mathrm{rank}(A)=2$.**

---

### Q7

Find the rank by row reduction:

```math
A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 & 0 \\ 4 & -2 & 1 \\ 6 & -3 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\,.
```

**Concept.** Row operations preserve rank; zeros expose dependent rows.

**Solution (step by step).**

1. $R_2\leftarrow R_2-2R_1$: $[0,0,1]$.

2. $R_3\leftarrow R_3-3R_1$: $[0,0,1]$.

3. $R_3\leftarrow R_3-R_2$: third row zero. Two independent nonzero rows $\Rightarrow$ **rank $2$.**

4. Note $\mathrm{row}_3=\mathrm{row}_1+\mathrm{row}_2$.

---

### Q8

For what values of $k$ does the matrix have rank $2$?

```math
A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 2 & 4 & k \\ 1 & 2 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\,.
```

**Concept.** Rows 1 and 3 are identical; rank drops further if row 2 aligns with row 1.

**Solution (step by step).**

1. Rows 1 and 3 equal $\Rightarrow$ $\mathrm{rank}\le 2$ always.

2. If $k=6$, $\mathrm{row}_2=2\,\mathrm{row}_1$ $\Rightarrow$ **rank $1$.**

3. If $k\neq 6$, row 2 is not a multiple of row 1 $\Rightarrow$ **rank $2$.**

**Answer:** $k\neq 6$.

<h2 id="sec4">4. Linear dependence of matrices</h2>

Work in $V=\mathbb{R}^{2\times 2}$ (dimension $4$).

### Q9

Are these matrices linearly independent?

```math
A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix},\quad
B = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix},\quad
C = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\,?
```

**Concept.** Matrices are **vectors** in $\mathbb{R}^4$ (four entries). Independent means only $aA+bB+cC=0$ with $a=b=c=0$.

**Solution (step by step).**

1. $aA+bB+cC=\begin{bmatrix}a+c&b+c\\b+c&a+c\end{bmatrix}$.

2. Zero matrix $\Rightarrow$ $a+c=0$, $b+c=0$ $\Rightarrow$ $b=a$, $c=-a$.

3. Example $a=b=1$, $c=-1$ works. **Linearly dependent.**

---

### Q10

Are these linearly dependent?

```math
A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix},\quad
B = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix},\quad
C = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\,.
```

**Concept.** Dependent if one matrix is a combination of others.

**Solution (step by step).**

$A+B=C$ entrywise, so $A+B-C=0$. **Linearly dependent.**

---

### Q11

Find $a,b,c$ with $aA+bB+cC=0$ for

```math
A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix},\quad
B = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix},\quad
C = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 \end{bmatrix}\,.
```

**Concept.** Four entries $\Rightarrow$ four linear equations in $a,b,c$.

**Solution (step by step).**

1. $(1,1)$: $a+2c=0$ $\Rightarrow$ $a=-2c$.

2. $(2,2)$: $4a+5c=0$ $\Rightarrow$ $-8c+5c=0$ $\Rightarrow$ $c=0$, then $a=0$.

3. $(1,2)$: $2a+b+3c=0$ $\Rightarrow$ $b=0$.

**Only** $a=b=c=0$. **Linearly independent.**
